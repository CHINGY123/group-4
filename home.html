LifeLabs: Typhoon Preparedness

Simple Actions to Keep Our Community Safe

Impacts of Typhoons

Homes and buildings are destroyed by strong winds.

Flooding damages crops and food supply.

Power outages and water shortages affect communities.

Diseases spread due to contaminated floodwaters.

Proposed Solution to Mitigate the Impact of Typhoons

Build strong and elevated evacuation centers in safe locations. These centers should be made of typhoon-resistant materials and placed on higher ground to keep people safe from strong winds and flooding during typhoons.

Why This Solution Matters

  • Provides a safe shelter for families during typhoons
  • Reduces the risk of injuries and deaths from flooding and strong winds
  • Helps communities recover faster after disasters

Other Important Features of Safe Evacuation Centers

  • Accessible to the elderly, children, and persons with disabilities
  • Stocked with emergency kits (water, food, medicine, flashlights, etc.)
  • Designed with proper ventilation, toilets, and clean water access
  • Built with solar panels or backup generators for electricity

What Communities Can Do

  • Participate in evacuation drills and disaster preparedness training
  • Support local leaders in building and maintaining safe shelters
  • Spread awareness about the importance of disaster-ready infrastructure
